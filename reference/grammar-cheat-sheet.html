Grammar Cheat Sheet

English for German Speakers · Growing document · Updated 2026-06-19

1. Third Person Singular -s

he / she / it + verb-s (or -es for go→goes, watch→watches; -ies for study→studies)
EnglishGerman parallel
He playsEr spielt (-t!)
She goesSie geht
It worksEs funktioniert

2. Present Continuous (am/is/are + -ing)

I am reading. He is reading. We are reading.

For actions happening right now. German doesn't have this form directly — it just uses present tense with "gerade".

3. Present Perfect (have/has + past participle)

I have seen. She has been. They have gone.

Similar to German Perfekt, but simpler: no "sein" for movement verbs. English always uses have. Never "is been" — always "has been".

4. First Conditional (if + present, will + verb)

If it rains, I will stay home. (NOT: If it *will rain)

Unlike German (Wenn es regnen wird), English never uses "will" in the if-clause.

5. Word Order: Subject–Verb–Object

English: For me, friends are important. (SVO)
German: Für mich sind Freunde wichtig. (V2)

German moves the verb to position 2. English does not — subject stays before verb even after an introductory phrase.
