0001 — The Third Person -s

📚 Grammar⏱ ~12 min🎯 A1 → A2🇩🇪→🇬🇧 German speaker

English verbs are mostly simple. I drink, you drink, we drink, they drink. No changes! But there is one exception — and that's what this lesson is about.

Englische Verben ändern sich fast nie. Nur bei einer Person kommt ein -s dran. Das ist die ganze Regel.
英语动词几乎不变。只有一个人称要加 -s。就这一条规则。

The Rule

If the subject can be replaced by he, she, or it → add -s to the verb.
he / she / it → Verb bekommt -s. Genau wie im Deutschen er/sie/es ein -t bekommt!
he/she/it → 动词加 -s。就像德语里 er/sie/es 加 -t 一样！
SubjectVerbGerman (zum Vergleich)
I drinkdrinkich trinke
You drinkdrinkdu trinkst
We drinkdrinkwir trinken
They drinkdrinksie trinken
He / She / It drinksdrinkser/sie/es trinkt ← Merk dir das -t!
💡 Memory hook: You already know when to change the verb — from German (er trinkt). You just need to switch the ending from -t to -s. Same logic, different letter.
When I first learned this as a kid, my dad (the Cambridge professor) said: "The -s is just the verb tipping its hat to he, she, and it. Very British of it." I still think of it that way. 🎩

Three Spelling Patterns

Most verbs just add -s. But three groups have special rules:

TypeRuleExample
Most verbsAdd -splay → plays, work → works, speak → speaks
Ending in -o, -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -zAdd -esgo → goes, watch → watches, miss → misses
Ending in consonant + yy → iesstudy → studies, cry → cries
⚠️ Achtung: play → plays (nicht *plaies). Weil ay = Vokal+y. Die y→ies-Regel gilt nur bei Konsonant+y.
play → plays（不是 *plaies）。因为 ay 是元音+y。只有在辅音+y 时才变成 ies。

Nouns Are Third Person Too

Doesn't matter if the subject is a pronoun (he, she, it) or a full noun — the rule is the same:

Subject→ Replace withVerb
My friendhe / sheplays
This methoditworks
The catitsleeps
The universityitoffers
My mothershecooks
Ob he oder my friend — die Regel ist dieselbe. Kannst du das Subjekt mit he/she/it ersetzen? Dann kommt -s.
不管主语是 he 还是 my friend —— 规则一样。能用 he/she/it 替换吗？那就加 -s。

🇩🇪→🇬🇧 Your German Superpower

These words are nearly identical in German and English. You already know them — you just need the English spelling:

Here's the thing most English teachers won't tell you: if you speak German at C1, you already know about 30% of English vocabulary. The words are just wearing different clothes. Wasser is water, Methode is method, Information is information. Your brain has these stored — we just need to put the English labels on them. This is your superpower. Use it. 🦸
